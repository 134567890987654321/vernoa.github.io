7. Supply-Side Effects

Lower marginal tax rates can raise potential GDP by increasing labor supply, saving & productivity.

‚Üí Rightward shift of LRAS

8. Ricardian Equivalence

If households view today's deficit as tomorrow's taxes, they save the tax cut ‚Üí AD unchanged.

9. Debt & Sustainability

Debt-to-GDP = (Debt / Nominal GDP) √ó 100. Persistent deficits raise this ratio, risking higher future taxes or default premiums.

10. Problems: The Three Lags

1. Recognition Lag

The time it takes to realize a recession or inflation is actually happening (due to data delays).

2. Administrative Lag

The time it takes for Congress/President to agree on and pass the new legislation (politics).

3. Operational Lag

The time it takes for the spending to actually affect the economy (planning, building, etc.).
