1. Definition

Fiscal Policy = deliberate changes in:

  • (a) Government Purchases (G)
  • (b) Net Taxes (T)

made by the federal government to influence AD, output, employment, and the price level.

2. Types & Direction

  • Expansionary (G‚Üë or T‚Üì):
    AD‚Üë ‚Üí Real GDP‚Üë, UE‚Üì, PL‚Üë
  • Contractionary (G‚Üì or T‚Üë):
    AD‚Üì ‚Üí Real GDP‚Üì, UE‚Üë, PL‚Üì
  • Discretionary: New legislative action.
  • Automatic Stabilizers: Existing rules (progressive tax, UE benefits) that swing budget toward deficit in recession & surplus in expansion.

4. Automatic Stabilizers Logic

Economic Boom (Overheating)

Starting Condition: Real GDP ‚Üë, Unemployment ‚Üì

‚Üí Government Transfers ‚Üì
Fewer people qualify for unemployment benefits, welfare payments, food stamps, etc.

‚Üí Tax Revenue ‚Üë
Higher incomes push people into higher tax brackets. More people working = more income tax collected.

‚Üí Budget Deficit ‚Üì
Balance = T ‚Äì G ‚Äì TR improves automatically.

RESULT: CONTRACTIONARY
Dampens the boom

Recession (Underheating)

Starting Condition: Real GDP ‚Üì, Unemployment ‚Üë

‚Üí Government Transfers ‚Üë
More people qualify for unemployment benefits, welfare, food stamps, disability. Gov't pays out more automatically.

‚Üí Tax Revenue ‚Üì
Lower incomes push people into lower tax brackets. Fewer people working = less income tax collected.

‚Üí Budget Deficit ‚Üë
Balance = T ‚Äì G ‚Äì TR worsens automatically.

RESULT: EXPANSIONARY
Supports the economy

5. Output & Employment

  • Expansionary Fiscal: Output ‚Üë and Unemployment ‚Üì (short-run)
  • Contractionary Fiscal: Output ‚Üì and Unemployment ‚Üë (short-run)
  • Use when Real GDP < Full Employment (recessionary gap) ‚Üí Expansionary
  • Use when Real GDP > Full Employment (inflationary gap) ‚Üí Contractionary

6. Budget Balances

Budget = T ‚Äì G ‚Äì TR (Taxes minus Government Purchases minus Transfers)
  • Balanced: T = G + TR
  • Deficit: T < G + TR (Government spending > Tax revenue)
  • Surplus: T > G + TR (Tax revenue > Government spending)

7. Crowding Out

When government borrows to finance deficit spending:

Budget Deficit ‚Üë ‚Üí Demand for Loanable Funds ‚Üë ‚Üí Interest Rate ‚Üë ‚Üí Private Investment ‚Üì

Effect: Reduces the fiscal multiplier.

Note: More crowding out = lower multiplier effect on AD.

8. Supply-Side Effects

  • Tax Cuts on Productivity: Can shift SRAS right (supply-side boost)
  • Infrastructure Spending: Increases productive capacity ‚Üí SRAS shifts right
  • Long-run (LRAS): Fiscal policy doesn't permanently shift LRAS in Keynesian model

9. The Three Lags

Recognition Lag: Time to identify the problem (e.g., recession)

Implementation Lag: Time for legislation to pass and spending to begin

Impact Lag: Time for fiscal stimulus to work through the economy

Total: Often 1‚Äì2 years. By then, the recession may have ended!
